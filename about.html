About Goldie

My Story

Hi, I'm Goldie Zhu. I'm a Chinese-American who grew up in the suburbs of the Bay Area, California. Growing up, I dabbled in a lot of extracurricular activities, such as sports, music, art, and debate. This variety of influences and exposures in my childhood led me to be more inclined towards multidisciplinary, human-related studies. I am deeply fascinated by any studies related to cognitive science, philosophy, public policy, and power dynamics.

During my senior year of high school, I made the impulsive decision to leave my small suburban bubble and go to college in China, which was quite a dramatic change in perspective, lifestyle, and environment. Through the good and the bad of seeing a different side of the world, a lot of people at Tsinghua University helped me grow and mature into who I am today. It was truly a life changing experience that motivated me to become more career driven and aspirational, but I decided that computer science wasn't a path that I'd be happy pursuing.

This led me to where I am today: a happy transfer student at the University of Toronto School of Information! I think I've finally found something I really enjoy doing and after graduation, I'm considering going to grad school to continue my studies on the influence of information and technology on politics, power, and human rights.
